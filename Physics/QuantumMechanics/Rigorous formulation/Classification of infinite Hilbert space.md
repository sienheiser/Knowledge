Claim: Any seperable infinite dimension Hilbert space is unitarily isomorphic to the seperable Hilbert space $(\mathcal{l}^2(\mathbb{N}),+,\cdot,\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle)$  where 
$$
\mathcal{l}^2(\mathbb{N}) = \{a:\mathbb{N}\rightarrow \mathbb{C}|\sum_{n}|a_n|^2 < \infty\}.
$$
Basically the set of all square summable sequences.

We define the norm of this space to be 
$$
\langle a,b\rangle := \sum_n^\infty \bar{a}_nb_n.
$$
